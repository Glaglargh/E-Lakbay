PUBLIC BATH IN MAUBAN

The historic public bath in Mauban, Quezon is a remarkable testament to the town's rich colonial heritage. Built during the Spanish colonial period, this architectural gem served as a communal bathing facility where locals gathered not only for hygiene but also for social interaction.

The structure features traditional Spanish colonial design elements, including coral stone walls and intricate water distribution systems that showcase the engineering ingenuity of the era. Today, it stands as one of Mauban's most significant cultural landmarks, offering visitors a glimpse into the daily life and social customs of Filipinos during the colonial period.

Through E-LAKBAY's AR technology, you can now explore this historic site in unprecedented detail, viewing reconstructed scenes and learning about its fascinating history interactively.

ABOUT THE PROJECT

E-LAKBAY was developed by a dedicated team of researchers and developers passionate about preserving and promoting Philippine cultural heritage through innovative technology. In order to promote tourism in Mauban, Quezon, and raise awareness about its rich historical significance, we created this augmented reality platform that allows users to experience heritage sites in an immersive and interactive way. By leveraging cutting-edge AR technology, we aim to attract more visitors, educate the younger generation about their cultural roots, and contribute to the preservation of these invaluable historical landmarks while creating sustainable opportunities for local communities.
